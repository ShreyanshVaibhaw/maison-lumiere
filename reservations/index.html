Before you book

An evening worth planning for

Dinner at Maison Lumière is meant to be unhurried, so we keep the room small and the pace gentle. That also means tables are limited, and the most-requested evenings - Friday and Saturday, and any night during the height of the season - tend to fill within days of opening. Booking a little ahead is always wise, and we are happy to suggest a quieter midweek evening if your dates are flexible.

The form below sends a request to our maître d', who confirms each booking by hand. Tell us as much as you like about the occasion and any dietary needs, and we will tailor the evening accordingly. Most guests stay with us for two to three hours, so plan to settle in, let the courses arrive at their own rhythm, and watch the light fade over the harbour.

Good to know

Booking policy

This form sends a request rather than an instant confirmation. Our maître d' reviews each booking by hand and replies by email within one day, usually much sooner.

Holding your table

Tables are held for fifteen minutes past the reserved time. If your plans change, a quick note to our team the day before is always appreciated and keeps a seat open for another guest.

Larger parties

Groups of seven or more are welcome and best arranged through our private events team, who can plan a set menu and a wine pairing for the table.

The chef's counter

Four seats at the counter are released each evening for guests who want to watch the kitchen at work. Request them above and we will do our best to oblige.

Children & access

Well-behaved young guests are welcome at the earlier sittings. Our entrance on Rue Albany is step-free and the dining room is wheelchair accessible.
