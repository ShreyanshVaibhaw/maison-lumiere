The head chef

Camille Rousseau

Co-founder and head chef of Maison Lumière, cooking the coast of Marenport since 2009.

Biography

From Lyon to the Marenport quay

Camille trained in the bouchons of Lyon before spending six restless years in the kitchens of Provence and the Basque coast, where she learned that the best ingredient is rarely the rarest - it is the one closest to home and most carefully chosen.

She arrived in Marenport in 2008 looking for a season's work and never left. Within a year she and sommelier Tomas Vance had opened the doors of a former chandlery, and Maison Lumière was born. Camille still writes the menu by hand each afternoon, and still tastes every sauce before service.

Her cooking has been recognised by the regional culinary guild three years running, though she is quick to point out that the praise belongs to the coast as much as the kitchen. "We are stewards of what the tide brings," she says. "Our job is to get out of its way."

Philosophy

"Cook the place, not the trend"

For Camille, every dish should answer a single question: where are we, and what is this season offering us? A plate that could appear anywhere has, in her view, failed before it leaves the pass.

In her words

Four convictions

Season over surprise

Novelty fades by the second course. A tomato at the height of August needs almost nothing from me, and that restraint is the whole craft. I would rather serve the obvious thing perfectly than the clever thing carelessly.

The hands matter

I know the diver who brings our scallops and the family who grows our stone fruit. Those relationships are not marketing - they are how the food gets better. Trust travels up the chain and lands on the plate.

Waste is a failure of imagination

Bones become stock, trim becomes terrine, the herb stems perfume the oil. A small kitchen cannot afford waste, and a thoughtful one should not want to. Frugality and elegance are the same discipline.

Calm is the secret ingredient

A frantic kitchen tastes frantic. We keep the pass quiet, the pace steady, and the music low. Guests can feel the difference even when they cannot name it - that ease is what they remember.

At the counter

Dine where Camille cooks

A handful of seats at the chef's counter are held each night for guests who want to watch the kitchen at work.
