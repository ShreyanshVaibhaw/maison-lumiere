Our story

A small room with a long horizon

Maison Lumière began as a single sunlit kitchen on the Marenport quay. Fifteen years later, the horizon is still our larder and our compass.

2009

How we began

In the spring of 2009, chef Camille Rousseau and her partner, sommelier Tomas Vance, took the lease on a faded chandlery at the end of the quay. They kept the worn oak floor, hung a hundred candles, and opened with twelve tables and a menu that changed every single night.

Word travelled slowly, the way good things do in a harbour town. Fishermen began saving their best catch. A neighbour offered the herbs from her terrace. Within a year the chandlery had become a destination, and the name we chose - Maison Lumière, the house of light - had earned itself.

What we believe

Three principles

The coast decides

Our menu follows the boats, the tide, and the kitchen garden. If the morning brings nothing worthy of you, that dish simply waits for another day. We would rather change the card than compromise the plate.

Technique in service

Classical French method is our grammar, not our showpiece. A perfect beurre blanc or a clean consommé should disappear into pleasure, never announce itself. We cook to delight, not to impress.

Genuine welcome

Hospitality is the dish we are proudest of. From the first glass of something cold to the last candle guttering, we want every guest to feel expected, unhurried, and entirely at home.

The maison today

Thirty-eight seats, one rhythm

Today the dining room seats thirty-eight, and the kitchen is still small enough that every plate passes under the chef's eye. We grow our soft herbs and edible flowers on the terrace above the harbour, and we buy the rest from the same handful of farmers, divers and foragers we have known for years.

An evening at Maison Lumière unfolds slowly and deliberately. There is no rush to turn the table, no script at the door - only the quiet confidence that comes from doing one thing, in one room, for a very long time.
