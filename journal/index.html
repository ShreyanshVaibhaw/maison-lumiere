Why we write

The story behind the plate

A menu only ever shows you the destination - the finished dish, the price beside it, the wine that might accompany it. The journal is where we record the journey: the crate of figs that arrived before dawn, the diver who stayed ashore on a rough morning, the cold snap that quietly rewrote a week of service. These are the small decisions that never reach the printed card, and yet they shape everything you taste.

We write a few times each season, usually when something on the coast insists on being remembered. There is no schedule and no formula, only an honest attempt to explain how a small restaurant at the end of a quay decides what to cook, who to trust, and why some things are worth doing slowly. We hope these notes deepen the meal a little - and that they read just as well from an armchair as they do from the table.
