What you will see

Light, the sea, and the table

It is difficult to photograph a feeling, and what we hope you feel at Maison Lumière is hard to frame: the hush of a candlelit room, the smell of brown butter drifting from the pass, the particular blue the harbour turns just before service begins. Still, these images give a sense of the place we have spent fifteen years building at the end of the Marenport quay.

You will find the dining room in its evening dress, the open kitchen where every plate passes under the chef's eye, and the terrace garden that supplies our soft herbs and edible flowers. There are a few of the season's plates as well, from the seared scallops that open many a meal to the vanilla soufflé that so often closes it. None of these pictures is staged for an audience; each is simply a quiet record of an ordinary, careful evening.

Because our menu changes with the tide and the harvest, the plates pictured here may not appear on the night you visit - and that is rather the point. What stays constant is the room, the welcome, and the unhurried rhythm of the evening. Consider these images an invitation rather than a promise: come and see which version of the season the kitchen is cooking the night you join us.
